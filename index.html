Why The 50K Target Is Tight

A sub-6 50K requires 7:12/km average. That is close to the sub-5 marathon pace of 7:06/km, but over a longer distance. Desert exposure, sand, wind, heat, and support gaps make the target execution-first, not pace-chasing-first.

Adventure Race Framework

These are optional "fun" races, not KC qualifiers. They should be judged by whether they add durability, confidence, and mountain handling without stealing recovery from VDHM, the December marathon, or the 50K qualifier.

Evaluation Rules

  • Calendar cost: any trail race inside 3 weeks of VDHM or the marathon should be run conservatively or skipped.
  • Effort distance: estimate trail load as distance plus 1 km for every 100 m of climb. A 20 km trail with 1,500 m gain behaves more like a 35 km road effort.
  • Surface cost: sand, wet rock, forest trail, and steep descents matter more than distance because they raise fall and quad-damage risk.
  • Cutoff realism: the target is a comfortable finish, not racing. If cutoff requires pushing near marathon effort, it is not serving the plan.
  • Training role: choose one primary benefit: climbing strength, downhill skill, fueling practice, or mental confidence.

How To Use Them

  • Keep these as B/C events: hike climbs, run flats/easy descents, and finish with legs intact.
  • No taper longer than 2-3 easy days unless the race replaces a planned long run.
  • No all-out downhill running before VDHM or Goa; eccentric damage is the main hidden cost.
  • Carry mandatory kit even when aid is available: hydration, calories, shell, headlamp if early start, phone, ID, and basic first aid.
