Athlete Context

Current State

  • Restarting after roughly 7 months of detraining.
  • Current limiter is structural tolerance, not motivation.
  • Lower back, hips, glutes, and posterior chain need reloading.
  • Running remains the KC priority, but the athlete should stay hybrid / triathlon-oriented.

Early Risk Flags

  • Barbell rows, heavy deadlifts, and high-fatigue hinge work are not early defaults.
  • Back-safe strength work is required, not optional.
  • The first 60 days are an eligibility test for durability.

Core Philosophy

Order Of Priority

  1. Durability first: back, hips, calves, trunk, glutes.
  2. Run consistency second: weekly frequency and long-run progression.
  3. Aerobic support third: bike and swim without run compromise.
  4. Strength always: full-body work adjusted around back tolerance.

Weekly Question

Did this week make the next week easier to absorb, or did it create debt?

If it created debt, do not scale.

Weekly Build Formula

1. Score The Current Week

Back/hip, long-run recovery, easy-run feel, strength response, and life load get a green/yellow/red status.

2. Choose The Next Week

All green moves forward. One yellow repeats or trims. Two yellows hold. Any red cuts volume 15-25%.

3. Add Workouts Later

Before 60 km, the only regular quality is strides. Threshold is earned, not assumed.

Strength Philosophy

Session Structure

  1. Prep: mobility + activation
  2. Power primer: light jumps, skips, hops, or strides
  3. Main lower-body pattern
  4. Upper push / upper pull
  5. Accessory / trunk
  6. Exit check: back and hips should not feel worse

Early Block Bias

  • Split squats, step-ups, hip thrusts, goblet squats, chest-supported rows, cable rows, pull-ups, presses, carries, dead bugs, side planks, and bird dogs.
  • Avoid heavy bent-over rows and heavy deadlifts until the back is stable.
  • Keep hinge work light and controlled at first.

First 4-Week Strength Block

Use two full-body strength sessions per week. Keep 2-3 reps in reserve. Nothing should be a grinder.

Week Progression
1Learn positions. Conservative load. Stop every set with clean reps left.
2Add 1-2 reps per set or a small load increase if back is quiet.
3Add load modestly on main patterns. Keep rows supported.
4Deload 20-30% or keep load but reduce one set per movement.

Bike And Swim Role

Bike

Use bike for aerobic volume when legs need lower impact. Avoid long bike sessions that provoke lower-back soreness.

Swim

Swim stays in the mix Monday evening through Friday morning and becomes more important as Goa approaches.

Race-Specific Priorities

  • Keep most running easy.
  • Practice fueling every 30-35 minutes on long runs.
  • Practice salt and fluid intake in heat.
  • Use back-to-back durability only once 70-80 km weeks are normal.
  • Jaisalmer is qualification first: 5:50-5:59 is the execution window.

Red Flags

  • Back/hip pain changes gait.
  • Long-run soreness lasts beyond 48 hours.
  • Easy runs feel like moderate runs for more than two days.
  • Strength work creates next-day back symptoms.
  • A planned 80 km week requires squeezing runs into bad recovery windows.
